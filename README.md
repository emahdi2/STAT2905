  <h1>STAT 2509 — Introduction to Statistical Modeling II</h1>

  <p>
    <strong>Course Repository for STAT 2509</strong><br>
    Course title: <em>Introduction to Statistical Modeling II</em>
  </p>

  <div class="section">
    <h2>📘 About the Course</h2>
    <p>
      STAT 2509 is an intermediate-level statistics course that builds on foundational
      statistical methods and introduces key modeling techniques used in data analysis
      and experimental inference. Students learn to construct, evaluate, and interpret
      statistical models for data from both designed experiments and observational studies.
    </p>
    <p>
      The course emphasizes both theory and applications, including hands-on practice
      with statistical software (e.g., R, SAS, or similar).
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="section">
    <h2>🧠 Course Description</h2>
    <p>
      This course provides a data-driven approach to statistical modeling. Topics include
      the estimation and inference of regression models, analysis of variance (ANOVA) for
      various experimental designs, and methods for analyzing categorical outcomes.
    </p>
    <p>
      Students also learn residual diagnostics, model selection, and interpretation of
      model results in real-world settings.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="section">
    <h2>📚 Topics Covered</h2>
    <ol>
      <li>
        <strong>Simple Linear Regression</strong>
        <ul>
          <li>Least squares estimation</li>
          <li>Inference for regression coefficients</li>
          <li>ANOVA for regression</li>
          <li>Model diagnostics and residual analysis</li>
        </ul>
      </li>

      <li>
        <strong>Multiple Linear Regression</strong>
        <ul>
          <li>Least squares in multivariate settings</li>
          <li>Inference and interpretation of coefficients</li>
          <li>ANOVA for multiple regression</li>
          <li>Multicollinearity and variable selection</li>
        </ul>
      </li>

      <li>
        <strong>Completely Randomized Design (CRD)</strong>
        <ul>
          <li>One-way ANOVA</li>
          <li>Treatment effects and F-tests</li>
        </ul>
      </li>

      <li>
        <strong>Randomized Block Design (RBD)</strong>
        <ul>
          <li>Blocking to reduce experimental variability</li>
          <li>ANOVA for block designs</li>
        </ul>
      </li>

      <li>
        <strong>Two-Factor Experimental Designs</strong>
        <ul>
          <li>Two-way ANOVA</li>
          <li>Main effects and interaction effects</li>
        </ul>
      </li>

      <li>
        <strong>Analysis of Categorical Data</strong>
        <ul>
          <li>Contingency tables</li>
          <li>Pearson’s Chi-square tests</li>
          <li>Tests of independence and homogeneity</li>
        </ul>
      </li>
    </ol>
  </div>
